"Legal Concerns"

Ethical Concerns Social Concerns Legal Concerns Bibliography

During software development, programmers often find themselves confronted by a myriad of legal considerations,
with each regulation holding significant implications for both users, and creators. These considerations
must be followed to ensure the integrity, and compliance of code, that not only abides by laws and regulations,
but is also justly moral.

Programmers should structure their code, around the following most widely known, and encountered regulations to
ensure they program, and publish an application that utilizes legally moral code:

Intellectual Property:

Developers should ensure they adhere to intellectual property regulations by not using copyrighted material, including code,
images, audio, or software without proper authorization. They could potentially structure their code to include primarily open
source assets and follow GNU licensing terms for free libraries to avoid copyright infringement issues.

Software Patents:

Expanding on intellectual property rights, developers can structure their code to ensure it doesnt too include potentially
patented software or technology without proper liscensing or permissions. To avoid this they should conduct thorough web
searches for patents utilizing the existing technology to identify potential conflicts prior to implementation, or development.

Ownership:

Software development often involves multiple programmers collaborating on one project. Developers should establish clear
ownership agreements amongst their development team to avoid any potential miscommunication or disagreement. Creating repositories
and version control systems to track ownership would too help prevent disputes.

Trade Secrets:

Protecting trade secrets involves keeping sensitive information confidential. The privacy of a users sensitive information
is of the utmost importance to a software developer, so they should structure their code to limit access to this data, and
implement enhanced cybersecurity procedures and algorithms to prevent unauthorized disclosure through cyberattacks.

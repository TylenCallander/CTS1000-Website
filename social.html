Privacy:

Privacy is not just a feature implemented into applications, it is a fundamental human right, integral to maintaining personal
autonomy and dignity in the digital age. Privacy in software development involves the safeguarding of user data from authorized
access and misuse, that ensures confidentiality,and integrity.

A study interview performed in 2012 involved 12 software engineers, who were drawn from various global regions and companies,
but all of which had an average experience of 10 years. The programmers described during their interviews that hurdles like
cultural resistance and technical landscapes when enforcing privacy, with their approaches including informal advocacy and
managerial collaboration. This highlights the necessity for organization support in privacy integration. Furthermore, the software
experts all view privacy as a “broad, complex, and contextual term”, mainly associating it with data protection through encryption
and cryptography. Not only did the results of this research underscore the crucial role of proactive privacy measures in software
development, but it also suggests that privacy and security are intertwined, as the two are at odds, necessitating a balance.

Inclusivity:

In software development, inclusivity entails coding software that is accessible to all demographics, regardless of its users race,
gender, sex or impairment. It should foster equal digital experiences, without discrimination, while simultaneously reflecting
a commitment to fairness and accessibility for all.

Research from 2022 titled, “Responsive inclusive design (RiD): a new model for inclusive software development" demonstrates
this necessity for inclusivity in coding to uphold morality throught the RiD model. The RiD model presents a model for
developing software that is inclusive for those with disabilities, which was applied to a study called EDICO. Due to the
implementation of the RiD, EDICO was downloaded over 7,000 times and installed in 26 faculties of the Complutense University
of Madrid during the time of the study. The utilization of a tool that promotes inclusivity ensured that the Spanish National
Organization of the Blind met software diversity needs, while RiD set a benchmark for inclusive design. The study showcased not
only amplified the benefits and recognition of this tool, but the data collected demonstrated the need for reinforcing such
inclusive practices in coding.

Mental Health:

Mental health impact is too a crucial, and increasingly pressing social concern, especially with the prevalence of social media,
and their capacity to influence, and addict youth.

A study conducted in 2019 explores this idea by analyzing the association between social media use and mental health issues
in teens in the US. The study assessed the self-reported time spent my teens on social media, and how it affected their
mental well-being. The results of the research concluded that teens who spent more than 3 hours on social media daily
indeed reported higher odds of internalizing and externalizing problems, which were amplified further if they had prior
history of mental health issues. While the results of this study do highlight the potential psychological risks of excessive
social media usage among teenagers, it can also be concluded that the need for interventions from software developers that
program these addictive applications should ensure their code promotes healthy user engagement, and does not exploit the
vulnerabilities of youth.
